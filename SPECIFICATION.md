# System Specification

## Project Title

Computer Repair Management System

## Domain

The system operates in the Information Technology (IT) support and computer repair domain. Computer repair shops often handle multiple customer devices for maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair services. Managing customer information, device details, accessories received with devices, and repair progress can become difficult when handled manually.

This system is designed to support computer repair businesses by providing a digital platform for recording customer devices, tracking repair progress, and managing repair-related information efficiently.

## Problem Statement

Many small computer repair businesses manage repair jobs manually using notebooks or spreadsheets. This approach can lead to lost records, poor tracking of repair progress, and confusion regarding accessories received with devices such as chargers or bags. It can also make it difficult to monitor repair pricing and calculate profit from repair services.

The purpose of the Computer Repair Management System is to provide a structured digital solution for managing repair jobs, tracking device information, recording accessories received, monitoring repair progress, and recording repair pricing.

## Individual Scope

This project focuses on developing the core functionality of a computer repair management system. The system will allow technicians to register customer devices, record device problems, track accessories received with devices, and monitor repair status from device intake to completion.

The system will also record service handling options, including whether the client will drop off the device, request technician pick-up, collect the device after repair, or request technician delivery. Additionally, the system will record repair pricing and calculate profit for each repair job.

The project will be implemented as a Rust-based application running on Ubuntu Linux. Advanced features such as online payments, customer portals, or automated messaging notifications are outside the scope of this project.
